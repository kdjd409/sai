1. **Accommodation**: The main purpose of hotels is to provide temporary lodging for travelers. This includes tourists, business travelers, families on vacation, and individuals needing a place to stay while away from home.

2. **Hospitality Services**: Hotels offer a range of services to make guests' stays comfortable and convenient. This includes housekeeping, room service, concierge services, and sometimes amenities like spas, fitness centers, and business centers.

3. **Meeting and Event Spaces**: Many hotels have facilities for conferences, meetings, weddings, and other events. These spaces can range from small meeting rooms to large ballrooms, accommodating various types of gatherings.

4. **Dining and Entertainment**: Hotels often have restaurants, bars, cafes, and lounges where guests can dine and socialize. Some hotels also offer entertainment options such as live music, cultural performances, or recreational activities.

5. **Business and Corporate Travel**: Hotels cater to business travelers by offering amenities like high-speed internet, work desks, meeting rooms, and proximity to business districts or conference centers.

6. **Tourism and Leisure Activities**: Hotels located in tourist destinations often provide information and assistance for sightseeing, tours, and local attractions, enhancing the overall travel experience for guests.

7. **Temporary Housing**: In some situations, hotels may serve as temporary housing for individuals or families who are between homes due to relocation, renovations, or other reasons.
